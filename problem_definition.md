# Problem Definition & Scoping: Fraud Detection

## 1. Business Objective
The primary business objective is to detect fraudulent transactions.

## 2. Success Metrics
The performance of the model will be evaluated based on the following metrics:
* **Precision:** High precision means fewer "false alarms" for legitimate customers.
* **Recall:** High recall means stopping more criminals.
* **F1-Score:** The harmonic mean of Precision and Recall. This serves as an "all-rounder" metric to balance the two.
* **Precision-Recall (PR) Curve:** Used as a primary visual metric (often better than ROC-AUC for imbalanced fraud data) to focus on the minority class.
* **False Positive Rate (FPR):** Must be kept low to reduce the "insult rate" where loyal users get frustrated by declined cards.
* **False Positive Ratio:** Evaluated as the ratio of legitimate users annoyed for every 1 actual fraud case caught.
* **Alert Volume:** Must remain manageable so that human investigators can handle the daily flags generated by the model.
* **Latency:** The model must execute in real-time. If the model takes too long, the transaction "times out," rendering the prediction useless regardless of its accuracy.

## 3. Machine Learning Task
* **Type:** Supervised binary classification.

## 4. Feasibility Assessment
* **Status:** Feasible. I have the right data and resources to solve this problem.
